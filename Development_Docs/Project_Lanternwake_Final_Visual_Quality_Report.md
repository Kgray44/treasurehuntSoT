## Visual closure decisions

- `AS-008`, `AS-009`, `AS-014`, `AS-015`, and `AS-017` are recorded as `superseded-approved`: their former standalone scenes are intentionally static tombstones because the visible production scenes own the corresponding effect without a duplicate runtime/property claim.
- `MX-261` and `OA-188` are recorded as `rejected-approved`: no microphone or required audio-reactive channel was introduced. The final mechanism remains semantically complete when sound is muted, blocked, or absent; this is the accepted accessible product behavior.

No visual acceptance relies on a completed timer alone. The validation report records the concrete browser surfaces and controls used for each observed result.
